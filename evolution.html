The progression of electronic games: From the first to the latest generation

Video games are projected to be worth US$ 100 billion per year in today's market. Households in the United States consume the majority of this product.


Electronic Game Development  

It is notable for its progression throughout the decades, from its inception with Arcade versions to modern mobile device models.

A continuous development that keeps the market moving.

Additional technologies are increasingly being incorporated into equipment, and electronic games are evolving to provide consumers with new options and capabilities.

Increasing the quality of the players' experience.

This has an impact on the entertainment sector since more and more games are turning into movies, and movies are turning into games.

Marketplace Old Video Games

Mega Drive is growing more and more.

A study conducted by a Brazilian e-commerce showed a 30% increase in searches for this type of product.

Below is a list of the most wanted classic consoles:

1. SUPER NINTENDO: Most famous console of the 80's generation, it still wins the hearts of fans thanks to charismatic heroes and creative storylines like Super Mario, Donkey Kong and Metroid. Value US$ 100.

2. PLAYSTATION ONE: Classic that revolutionized the console world in the 90's (lasting until 2006 when it was discontinued). It has popular titles like Resident Evil, Tomb Raider, Gran Turismo. Value US$ 100.

3. ATARI 2600: Who never heard PAC MAN, Breakout or Donkey Kong, all these games were released on the Atari 2600, marking the lives of players in the 70s and 80s. Value US$ 100.

4. MEGA DRIVE: Direct competitor of the Super Nintendo due to the similar performance of the games. It has the classics SONIC, Mortal Kombat 2 and 3. Value US$ 50

New Generations Video games

Although the previous two years have not been easy, this does not apply to the video game industry. During these years of epidemic and social isolation, many individuals went back into the habit of playing or started playing again.

The debut of the new generation Playstation 5 and XBOX X/S series was the golden key.

1. XBOX X/S: Microsoft's 9th-generation consoles are released. The XBOX X (top of the range) and XBOX S (entry-level) variants are available (best cost-benefit).

2. Playstation 5: There are two versions of this game: a disk version and a digital edition.

3. Games "In a Nutshell": In recent years, multiplayer games such as FALL GUYS, Among Us, and Animal Crossing have dominated the market.
